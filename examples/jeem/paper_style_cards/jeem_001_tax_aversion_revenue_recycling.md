## Style Card

| Dimension | Observed writing move |
|-----------|-----------------------|
| Abstract | Opens from a policy-instrument puzzle, then moves to design, comparison, and interpretation. |
| Introduction | Frames a familiar environmental-economics instrument through acceptance, understanding, and design. |
| Gap | The gap is not just policy effectiveness; it is why agents respond differently to economically similar instruments. |
| Contribution | Contribution is tied to experimental design and policy-design interpretation. |
| Method exposition | Explains the institutional setup before asking readers to interpret the behavioral mechanism. |
| Results style | Results are written as comparisons across treatments or policy frames, followed by interpretation. |
| Discussion | Returns to what the mechanism implies for policy design rather than making a broad environmental claim. |
| Language | Economics register with policy-accessible motivation; direct but bounded claims. |

## Useful Rule Extracted

When the manuscript studies a policy instrument, make the reader see the decision environment and behavioral or welfare mechanism before presenting results.

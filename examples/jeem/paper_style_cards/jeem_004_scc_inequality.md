## Style Card

| Dimension | Observed writing move |
|-----------|-----------------------|
| Abstract | Places a familiar climate-economics object inside a distributional or welfare-design frame. |
| Introduction | Opens with a high-level policy metric, then narrows to the welfare channel that changes its interpretation. |
| Gap | The gap is stated as a missing link between inequality, redistribution, and climate-price calculation. |
| Contribution | Contribution is tied to the welfare criterion and to how the model changes interpretation of a known object. |
| Method exposition | Defines welfare structure and redistribution channel before numerical or calibration discussion. |
| Results style | Comparisons are interpreted through welfare logic rather than policy appeal alone. |
| Discussion | Bounded policy interpretation; does not overgeneralize beyond the framework. |
| Language | Technical but readable; central concepts are repeated consistently. |

## Useful Rule Extracted

If the manuscript uses a welfare criterion, make that criterion visible early and let it organize both contribution and results.

## Style Card

| Dimension | Observed writing move |
|-----------|-----------------------|
| Abstract | Starts from a policy-preference puzzle and links it to a mechanism. |
| Introduction | Uses an intuitive policy contrast, then sharpens it into an economic explanation. |
| Gap | The gap is framed around why policy rankings or preferences differ under a specific salience channel. |
| Contribution | Contribution is tied to mechanism identification and policy-design interpretation. |
| Method exposition | Explains behavioral or salience channel before formal implications. |
| Results style | Organizes results around why one instrument differs from another. |
| Discussion | Keeps interpretation close to the mechanism rather than treating preference as a broad fact. |
| Language | Accessible opening, then economics-facing mechanism language. |

## Useful Rule Extracted

For policy-preference topics, do not stop at "people prefer X"; explain the mechanism that makes the preference economically relevant.
